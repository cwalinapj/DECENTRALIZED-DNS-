Domain Owner Flow

Switch your domain to TollDNS without changing registrars.

TollDNS is a portable naming and routing layer for web properties. The first product experience should be simple: switch nameservers, verify control, point traffic, and see continuity status clearly.

Read the switch guide Open nameserver onboarding Open the runtime control plane

Do not lead with protocol complexity. The conversion path is domain control, routing clarity, and continuity. Miners, token rails, and storage tracks are advanced background layers.

What changes

  • You point your nameservers to TollDNS.
  • You manage basic routing records through one clear path.
  • You get continuity and routing status in one place.

What does not change

  • Your registrar ownership stays with you.
  • You do not need a wallet to start.
  • You do not need to understand the protocol internals.

1. Connect Domain

Enter the domain, receive TollDNS nameservers, and see a short explanation of what the switch does.

2. Verify Ownership

Use nameserver propagation or a TXT challenge. Keep the flow explicit and reversible.

3. Route Traffic

Manage A, CNAME, and TXT records first. Everything more advanced should stay secondary.
